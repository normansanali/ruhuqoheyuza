Is 3 Mile Island still radioactive?

October 01, 2024

Technically Three Mile Island is still radioactive today but its levels of radiation are not believed to be dangerous to humans or nature, according to the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC).

Is Three Mile Island still active today?

No, Three Mile Island is not still operating today. The TMI-2 reactor was permanently shut down after the accident, with the reactor's coolant system drained, the radioactive water decontaminated and evaporated, and radioactive waste removed to an "appropriate disposal area," according to the NRC.

Is Three Mile Island still toxic?

Current Status. Today, the TMI-2 reactor is permanently shut down and 99% of its fuel has been removed. The reactor coolant system is fully drained and the radioactive water decontaminated and evaporated.

Is the Three Mile Island Nuclear Accident Site Safe Today? It is completely safe to visit (or live) in the area, today. In 1979, the plant leaked radioactive iodine 131 gases. How did they clean up Three Mile Island?

Three Mile Island Cleanup

The damaged reactor was permanently closed and entombed in concrete after the accident. Radioactive fuel and water were removed, and workers eventually shipped 15 tons of radioactive waste to a nuclear waste storage facility in Idaho.
