American rapper and musician Yung Bleu, whose real name is Jeremy Biddle, was born in Mobile, Alabama, on April 4, 1994. He started his music career in 2013, and a series of mixtapes helped him become well-known in the Southern music industry. With the release of his singles "Miss It" and "Ice on My Baby" from the mixtape Investments 4, which featured guest performances from YoungBoy Never Broke Again, Yung Bleu made a comeback in 2017. He later made remixes of "Miss It" and "Ice on My Baby" in 2018 with Kid Ink and Kevin Gates, respectively.

Yung Bleu's final recordings for Columbia Records and Bad Azz Music were the mixtape Inv 5 and the EP Bleu Vandross, both of which were released in 2020. The EP Love Scars: The 5 Stages of Emotions was released the same year after he obtained deals with Empire Distribution and Dream Chasers Records. The EP included Drake's remix of the Canadian rapper and singer's single "You're Mines Still," which peaked at number 18 on the Billboard Hot 100. Love Scars: The 5 Stages of Emotions by Yung Bleu was able to climb the Billboard 200 chart as a result.

The songs "Thieves in Atlanta" with Coi Leray, "Baddest" with Chris Brown and 2 Chainz, and "Way More Close (Stuck in a Box)" with Big Sean were all released by Yung Bleu in 2021. His debut album Moon Boy, which was published on July 23, 2021, contained all three of the tracks. Drake, Gunna, Kehlani, and more artists make cameos on the album as special guests.

The lead single from Yung Bleu's second studio album Tantra, which was released on November 11 of the same year, was "Love in the Way" with Nicki Minaj. Guests on the album include Fivio Foreign, Ty Dolla $ign, Ne-Yo, and others. In March 2023, he issued a follow-up EP titled Love Scars II, which featured Chris Brown, Tink, and Ty Dolla $ign as guests. The success of Yung Bleu's music career has led to a sizable fan base for him in the hip-hop and RnB music genres..
