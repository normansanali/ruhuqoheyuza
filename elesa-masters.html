DescriptionThis Electric-type Gym Leader also works as a model. Watching her battle in the beautiful, bright lights of her Gym can be mesmerizing.

Elesa (Japanese: カミツレ Kamitsure) is a Pokémon Trainer in Pokémon Masters EX, based on the character of the same name in the core series games. She forms a sync pair with Zebstrika. This sync pair was first made available on October 17, 2019. This pair could be raised to 6★ EX from October 2, 2020.

Elesa in her sygna suit forms a sync pair with Rotom. This sync pair was first made available on February 28, 2020. This pair could be raised to 6★ EX from January 7, 2021.

Elesa in her Classic attire forms a sync pair with Emolga, which is capable of Dynamaxing. This sync pair was first made available on December 9, 2021. This pair could be raised to 6★ EX from the day it was released.

Elesa also forms a sync pair with her Joltik. This pair can be obtained by reaching Friendship Level 100 with Elesa in the Trainer Lodge beginning on November 1, 2022. This pair could be raised to 6★ EX beginning on March 29, 2023, though this pair lacks an EX style.

Elesa in her Palentine's attire forms a sync pair with her Togetic. This pair was first made available through a seasonal sync pair scout beginning on February 2, 2023. This pair could be raised to 6★ EX from the day it was released.

Trivia

  • All versions of Elesa use special voice-over lines upon entering a team that contains certain Trainers (regardless of their partner Pokémon) and victory with them. Those Trainers include Skyla, Caitlin, Iris, and Nessa.
  • In the story event Chef's Heartful Delivery, Elesa reveals that her Togetic was raised from an Egg she received from Skyla.

Origin

Elesa's design is based on her appearance in Pokémon Black 2 and White 2. Zebstrika is Elesa's signature Pokémon in the core series games. Zebstrika's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Black 2 and White 2.

Elesa uses Joltik during her Gym battle against the player in Pokémon Black 2 and White 2 when playing on Challenge Mode. Joltik's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Black 2 and White 2.

Elesa is not seen to own a Rotom outside this game. Rotom's Dex entry comes from Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver.

Elesa's (Classic) design is based on her appearance in Pokémon Black and White. Elesa uses Emolga during her Gym battle with the player in the Unova-based core series games. Emolga's Dex entry comes from Pokémon Ultra Sun.
