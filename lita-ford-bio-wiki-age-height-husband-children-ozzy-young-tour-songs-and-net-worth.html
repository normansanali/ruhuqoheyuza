    Lita Ford Music

    Ford is a vastly recognized guitarist, vocalist, and songwriter. She was the former lead guitarist for the all-female rock band the Runaways. She is known for her successful glam metal solo career that hit its peak in the late 1980s.

    Likewise, Lita is an actress known for her roles in Rock ‘N Roll Sports, Highway to Hell, and The Hitchhiker. Afterward, she signed with Mercury Records and set about launching a solo career. Later, she made her debut solo album, Out for Blood, in 1983.

    Most noteworthy, Ford released Dancin’ on the Edge which included the single “Fire in My Heart”. Likewise, she recorded an unreleased album with RCA Records, and Tony Iommi did not perform on it.

    It is known that Lita signed with RCA Records, and hired Sharon Osbourne Management. As a result, she re-emerged with a more radio-friendly pop-metal sound. During this time, she released her most commercially successful album, the self-produced Lita.

    Furthermore, Ford’s album featured several singles such as Kiss Me Deadly, Back to the Cave, and Close My Eyes Forever. Her next release was Dangerous Curves, which featured her last charting single to date, “Shot of Poison”.

    On the other hand, Lita’s final album prior to a lengthy recording hiatus was Black on the German ZYX Records. Some of her popular songs are Back To The Cave, Falling in and out of Love, and Can’t Catch Me.
